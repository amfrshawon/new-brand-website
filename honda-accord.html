Honda Accord: Quick Specs

The Accord is a front-wheel-drive midsize sedan. The common 4-cylinder configuration runs a 1.5L–2.4L engine (depending on model year) producing roughly 160 hp, rated around 26 mpg city / 34 mpg highway, with a curb weight near 3,050 lbs and a 105–107" wheelbase. Retail price for a new Accord runs in the low-to-mid $20,000s depending on trim.

Trims We See Most

  • LX — base model, essential features and safety tech.
  • EX — adds comfort upgrades and extra convenience features.
  • Sport / Sport Hybrid — sportier styling, hybrid efficiency on Hybrid trims.
  • EX-L / EX-L Hybrid — premium interior and added technology.
  • Touring / Touring Hybrid — fully loaded, top trim.

Why Sell Your Accord Instead of Repairing or Trading It In

The Accord's reputation for reliability means a lot of them stay on the road well past 150,000 miles — but that also means many owners are staring down a transmission, timing, or hybrid-battery repair bill that costs more than the car is worth on a trade-in. Dealerships routinely lowball older Accords in trade because they're not chasing the sale on that specific car; a direct cash offer usually beats what a trade-in credit works out to, and you don't have to buy anything from us to get it.

Pros

Low cost of ownership, a comfortable and spacious cabin, strong resale value relative to other midsize sedans, and (on Hybrid trims) mileage that rivals much smaller cars.

Cons

No all-wheel-drive option, base LX trims are fairly bare-bones, and higher-mileage examples commonly need CVT or transmission attention — which is exactly the kind of repair bill that makes selling for cash the simpler option.
